Design of Radial Flux Outrunner BLDC Motor for Single-Rotor UAVs

Radial flux outrunner permanent magnet brushless DC (BLDC) motors find widespread applications in aerial robotics, particularly in unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). UAVs utilize either single or multiple BLDCs to generate enough thrust for takeoff. Single-rotor UAVs, like helicopters, employ a robust BLDC motor that operates at a fixed speed and manages the mechanical load simultaneously. This unique design sets it apart from conventional BLDC motors, making it more intricate. Despite the availability of various BLDC motor options tailored for different vehicle classes, there remains a need to comprehend and design a suitable BLDC motor for specific custom vehicles. The proposed study outlines the design principles of such a BLDC motor. The geometry of the motor is formulated through analytical calculations and refined using finite element analysis (FEA). Various analyses, including magnetostatic, transient, dynamic, and parametric simulations, are conducted to validate the analytical and FEA findings. Subsequently, a prototype is developed and tested in the laboratory to assess its suitability for single-rotor unmanned helicopters.

Keywords: Radial flux outrunner permanent magnet brushless DC, Single rotor UAV, Analytical design, Finite element analysis.
