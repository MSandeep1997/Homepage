Electromagnetic Refinement and Performance Characterization of a UAV BLDC Motor

This paper presents an electromagnetic finite element analysis study of a brushless DC motor utilizing the commercial software ANSYS Electronics Desktop. Key inputs for the electromagnetic modeling, including the stator, rotor core electrical steel, permanent magnet, and winding details, are identified through comprehensive testing, including a no-load test and generator mode test. By accounting for a rotational loss of $2\%$ and zero loss in the driving circuit during no-load operation, the core loss of the motor is determined, and an optimal electrical steel-magnet combination is selected to approximate the experimental no-load behavior. Additionally, the turn-per-phase information of the motor is calculated via iterative tuning of the winding and rate factor. Transient simulations are then conducted for two APC-series propellers, with simulation results effectively validating experimental findings. Finally, a performance plot of the motor is generated from the electromagnetic study, specifically tailored for constant load applications.

Keywords: Brushless DC, Electrical steel, Core loss, Finite element analysis.
